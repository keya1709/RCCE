Understanding the Carbon Market

The Voluntary Carbon Market (VCM) is a decentralized marketplace where organizations purchase carbon credits to offset their greenhouse gas emissions. Each credit represents exactly 1 metric ton of CO2 removed or avoided.

Reforestation is considered a premium "carbon removal" project because it actively captures carbon from the atmosphere through photosynthesis, storing it in biomass and soil for decades.

High-quality credits are built on principles of Additionality, Permanence, and Transparency—exactly what RCCE aims to provide.

Why it matters:

  • Verified Removal: Reforestation projects audited by standard bodies (like Verra or Gold Standard) ensure real impact.
  • Biodiversity: Restoring forests creates habitats for wildlife beyond just carbon storage.
  • Local Impact: Projects support soil health, water regulation, and community jobs.
  • Scalable Impact: VCM allows private capital to flow into critical ecosystem restoration.
